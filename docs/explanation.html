The Mayan Civilization is divided into three Periods by historians: The Pre-Classic Period from (1200B.C. – A.D. 250), the Classic Period from (A.D. 250 to 900 A.D.) and the Post-Classic period from (900 A.D. – 1508 A.D.) (Shoman, 2011, p.2). The Classic Period being the period where the Mayan Civilization according to Historians saw its peak and made great achievements. These great achievements were made in sectors that had appeared in the pre-Classic period such as in mathematics, astronomy, arts, architecture and writing and also the formation of a belief system (Shoman, 2011, p.2). Due to the advancements made by the Mayans, one of the most famed achievements made was the concept of the zero in mathematics. It was during this period that cities also began trading and some were able to control strategic and important trade routes which made them incredibly rich and powerful. Although they had a peak in their civilization it all came to a steady decline in the post-Classic period because of both external and internal factors, which were increase in wars, social upheaval, ecological failure, changes in trade patterns as well as the invasion of the foreigners from the west.
